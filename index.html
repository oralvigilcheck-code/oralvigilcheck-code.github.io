Oral Carcinoma Awareness

Oral carcinoma is a type of cancer that develops in the tissues of the mouth or oral cavity. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.

In India, oral cancer is among the top 3 cancers in males, with an estimated 77,000 new cases each year. Globally, over 350,000 new cases are reported annually.

  • • Persistent mouth sores or ulcers
  • • Red or white patches in the mouth
  • • Pain or difficulty in chewing/swallowing
  • • Numbness or swelling in the jaw
  • • Loose teeth without obvious cause

Regular self-examination helps identify suspicious lesions early. See a dentist immediately if you notice any persistent changes for more than two weeks.

  • • Visual and tactile examination
  • • Toluidine blue staining
  • • Brush biopsy / cytology
  • • Adjunctive light-based detection (ViziLite, Fluorescence imaging)
  • • Biopsy confirmation if required
